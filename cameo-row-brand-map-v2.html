Manchester, Jamaica
Generations rooted in the cool hills of Manchester Parish — once plantation country, now the fertile ground this brand grows from. The market row, the colonial townhouse door, the hummingbird that never stays still.

Akan · Ashanti · Ghana
DNA and slave ship records point to the Gold Coast — present-day Ghana — as the dominant African origin for Jamaicans. The Akan and Ashanti brought language, ceremony, and Adinkra: a visual language of proverbs and power.

Haiti
Haitian heritage brings the vèvè — sacred geometric symbols used in Vodou ceremony — and a tradition of ornate ironwork gates and sequined flags. The Haitian bicolour blue and red already lives inside your gradient palette.

Adinkra Symbols — Your Logo Language
Sankofa
"Go back and get it"
Return to roots to move forward. The founding symbol of this brand.
Adinkrahene
"Chief of Adinkra"
Greatness & leadership. The concentric oval echoes the cameo frame directly.
Nsoromma
"Child of the heavens"
Guardianship & divine guidance. A star that watches over the Row.
Dwennimmen
"Ram's horns"
Strength with humility. Power that does not announce itself.
The Central Thesis

The cowrie shell was African currency, adornment, and protection.
The cameo is a jewel carved from shell.
They are the same object — seen from two sides of history.

The Cameo Row has always existed — in the markets of Manchester, the vèvè-marked doors of Port-au-Prince, the gold-weighted courts of Ashanti. This is simply its current address.

World-Building & Lore
The Mythic Address
The Cameo Row has always existed — in the market rows of Manchester, the ironwork gates of Port-au-Prince, the gold-weighted courts of Kumasi. This is simply its current address.
Sankofa as Founding Principle
The brand looks backward to move forward. Every collection is an act of reclamation — beauty and craft that was always there, waiting to be named.
Guests, Not Shoppers
Customers are guests — received, not browsed. In the tradition of the market vendor who knows your name, who saves the good ones for you.
The Brand Voice
A letter from a grandmother with impeccable taste and deep memory. Knowing. Warm beneath the surface. Carrying stories it hasn't told yet.
